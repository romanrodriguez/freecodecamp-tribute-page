Dr. Terrence Sejnowski

Computational Neuroscience & Neural Networks Investigator and Professor

Dr. Terrence Sejnowski at the Salk Institute

Here's a brief timeline of Dr. Terry Sejnowski's life and contributions:

  • 1947 - Born in Cleveland, Ohio
  • 1968 - Received B.S. in Physics from the Case Western Reserve University
  • 1978 - Received M.A. in Physics and Ph.D. in Physics from Princeton University
  • 1984 - Received a Presidential Investigator Award from the National Science Foundation
  • 1985 - Co-invented the Boltzmann machine
  • 1989 - Founded Neural Computation, published by the MIT Press
  • 2008 - Elected to the Institute of Medicine
  • 2010 - Elected as a member to the National Academy of Sciences
  • 2011 - Elected to the National Academy of Engineering
  • 2011 - One of only twelve living scientists to have been elected to all 3 of the national academies
  • 2014 - Co-created and taught Learning How To Learn, the world's most popular online course
